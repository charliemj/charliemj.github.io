Charlie J. Moore

I’m Charlie and I’m a writer and software engineer.

I graduated from MIT with a double major in Computer Science and Engineering and Comparative Media Studies June 2017. I am currently the Chairman (Publisher/CEO) for MIT’s student newspaper, The Tech (previously a reporter and editor). I do my best to adhere to AP style guidelines (and to use the Oxford comma) and to thoroughly comment my code.

I have experience in full stack development and UI/UX design — I'll work on any part of the stack given the right project. I'm particularly excited about working on interdisciplinary projects related to the intersection of technology and society, especially journalism. I have experience teaching and I'm a good explainer. I am comfortable explaining complex ideas in simple terms to a variety of audiences — I work well in teams containing a mix of technical and non-technical folks.

I'm currently a fall software engineering intern at Datto Inc working on a cloud-based SaaS product for data backups. In this role, I'm learning Scala and Ruby, writing lots of tests, and squashing lots of bugs.

From fall 2017 to spring 2018, I served as a teaching assistant for MIT's Fundamentals of Programming (6.009), where I helped teach intermediate-level software engineering concepts using Python to over 400 students.

During the summer of 2017, I was a research intern on the Youth and Media and Artificial Intelligence teams at the Berkman Klein Center for Internet & Society at Harvard University in the summer of 2017. I wrote and designed educational resources to make the science behind AI technologies more accessible to non-technical audiences.

Check out my projects page to see more info about various software projects I've contributed to.

My hobbies include reading, making music, running, crafting, and watching science fiction TV shows.
