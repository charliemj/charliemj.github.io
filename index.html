Karleigh Moore

I’m Karleigh Moore and I’m a writer and software engineer currently based in Cambridge, MA. In June 2017, I’ll be graduating from MIT with a double major in Computer Science and Comparative Media Studies. In my free time, I am a reporter and editor for MIT’s newspaper, The Tech.

I’m interested in full stack development and UI/UX design, and I'm particularly interested in working on interdisciplinary projects related to the intersection of technology and society. Through my media studies coursework and research, I have experience with user design, visual design, and data visualization. On the engineering side, I have a background in programming, algorithms, and theoretical computer science.

I’ve worked as an undergraduate researcher in the Creative Communities Initiative group at MIT. My research focus was mixing quantitative research methods with ethnography in order to study fandom and fan behavior in online spaces.

I interned at Brilliant during the spring and summer of 2016 as a computer science writing intern. Brilliant is a company that hosts interactive educational STEM content for 4 million users worldwide. I wrote over 60 wiki pages teaching algorithms, theoretical computer science, and software concepts.
